DDoS Incident Response Tutor

Scenario: You are a cloud cybersecurity expert hired by an e-commerce company that relies heavily on its website for customer sales and support. Today, you receive a high-priority ticket: users are unable to access the website, reporting slow load times and frequent disconnections.

Detect DDoS Attack

Analyze the provided logs and traffic patterns to identify potential signs of a DDoS attack.

Time: 14:03 | IP: 192.168.1.1 | Requests: 300
Time: 14:04 | IP: 192.168.2.15 | Requests: 5000
...

Scale the Infrastructure

Choose appropriate actions to scale resources and ensure website availability during the attack.

  • Option 1: Add more virtual machines.
  • Option 2: Use auto-scaling tools provided by cloud providers.

Incorporate Mitigation Techniques

Implement rate limiting or enable managed DDoS protection to reduce attack impact.

  • Enable Rate Limiting
  • Enable Managed DDoS Protection

Release Resources

After the attack subsides, optimize resources by scaling back to reduce costs.

Monitor the traffic to ensure normalcy before reducing resources.
